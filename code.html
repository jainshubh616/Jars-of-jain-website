A Family's Legacy

Our Roots Where every jar begins — with love, legacy & a grandmother's kitchen

Behind every recipe is a story. Behind every jar is a family. This is ours — a journey of flavors passed down through generations, sealed with love and shared with you.

1970s

A Grandmother's Kitchen

It all started in a small kitchen in Bhopal, where our grandmother would wake before dawn to roast spices on a clay stove. The aroma would fill the entire neighbourhood — a silent invitation that needed no words. Her hands knew the perfect pinch, the right moment to stir, the patience to let flavors bloom.

1990s

Recipes Become Rituals

As children, we sat cross-legged on the kitchen floor, watching our mother replicate those same recipes with the same devotion. Festival mornings meant hand-rolling mathris while sharing stories. Every Sunday meant slow-frying banana chips until they turned to golden perfection. These weren't just snacks — they were how our family said "I love you."

2005

From Home to Your Home

When friends and family began asking us to share our homemade treasures, we realized these flavors deserved to travel beyond our kitchen. Jars of Jain was born — not as a business, but as a promise: to preserve the authenticity our grandmother taught us, one small batch at a time. Every jar we seal carries her blessing.

Today

Your Story Meets Ours

Twenty years later, the kitchen is bigger, but the heart is the same. We still roast spices by hand. We still count every jar. We still believe that food made with love tastes different — because it does. When you open a Jar of Jain, you're not just tasting a snack. You're tasting a family's love letter to tradition.

"We don't just make snacks. We preserve memories. Every jar is a bridge between the kitchen where it all began and the home where it's shared today."
— The Jain Family
